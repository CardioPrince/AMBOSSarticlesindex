PUD - GastroPrimeProtocol Summary

1. Disease Snapshot

Peptic Ulcer Disease is characterized by mucosal erosions ≥5 mm in the stomach or proximal duodenum caused by an imbalance between mucosal defenses and aggressive luminal factors like gastric acid or H. pylori. It typically presents with epigastric pain and can lead to complications such as bleeding, perforation, and gastric outlet obstruction.

2. Core Concepts Summary

  • Pathophys: H. pylori, NSAIDs, acid overproduction, impaired defenses
  • Risk Factors: H. pylori, NSAIDs, steroids, smoking, ZES
  • Symptoms: Epigastric pain (duodenal = better w/ food, gastric = worse), nausea, early satiety
  • Diagnosis: EGD + H. pylori testing
  • Treatment: Triple therapy (PPI + clarithro + amox/metronidazole); stop NSAIDs
  • Complications: GI bleed, perforation, GOO, malignancy

3. Mini Differentials

| Condition             | Key Differences from PUD                         |
|----------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Gastritis            | Diffuse inflammation, not focal ulcers          |
| GERD                 | Retrosternal burning, worse lying down          |
| Pancreatitis         | Radiating back pain, ↑ lipase/amylase           |
| Functional dyspepsia | No ulcer on EGD, no H. pylori                   |
| Gastric cancer       | Weight loss, anemia, mass on imaging            |

4. ABCv1.1 Flashcards

  • Definition: Mucosal defect ≥5 mm in stomach/duodenum
  • Causes: H. pylori, NSAIDs, ZES
  • Symptoms: Epigastric pain (gastric = worse w/ food, duodenal = better)
  • Alarm Signs: Bleeding, perforation, GOO, cancer
  • Diagnosis: EGD + H. pylori testing (urea/stool/biopsy)
  • Treatment: Triple therapy, stop NSAIDs, PPI maintenance if needed

5. Clinical Triggers

| Clinical Clue                         | Think PUD?    |
|--------------------------------------|---------------|
| Epigastric pain relieved by food     | Duodenal ulcer|
| Epigastric pain worsened by food     | Gastric ulcer |
| NSAID use + anemia                   | Gastric ulcer |
| Free air on X-ray                    | Perforation   |
| Vomiting + succussion splash         | GOO           |

6. Extra Section (Clinical Pearls)

  • Always biopsy gastric ulcers to rule out cancer
  • Triple therapy is 14 days; confirm eradication afterward
  • Refractory ulcers → think ZES or med nonadherence
