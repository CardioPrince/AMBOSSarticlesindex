GERD - GastroPrimeProtocol Summary

1. Disease Snapshot

GERD is a chronic condition in which stomach acid refluxes into the esophagus due to an incompetent lower esophageal sphincter (LES). It commonly presents with heartburn and regurgitation, worsens postprandially or when lying down, and may cause complications like esophagitis, Barrett’s esophagus, or strictures. Diagnosis is clinical in uncomplicated cases, and treatment typically involves lifestyle changes and PPIs.

2. Core Concepts Summary

  • Pathophysiology: LES dysfunction, hiatal hernia, obesity, delayed gastric emptying
  • Risk Factors: Obesity, pregnancy, smoking, alcohol, certain foods/meds
  • Symptoms: Heartburn, regurgitation, chest discomfort, hoarseness, cough
  • Diagnosis: Clinical ± EGD ± pH monitoring
  • Treatment: Lifestyle changes, PPIs (1st-line), H2 blockers, surgery (Nissen)
  • Complications: Esophagitis, Barrett’s, strictures, respiratory issues

3. Mini Differentials

| Condition             | How it Differs from GERD                            |
|----------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Angina/ACS           | Exertional, not postprandial; no relief with antacids|
| Esophageal spasm     | Chest pain + dysphagia, triggered by cold liquids    |
| Eosinophilic esophagitis | Young adults, atopy, strictures/rings on EGD    |
| PUD                  | Epigastric pain relieved by food                     |
| Achalasia            | Dysphagia to solids + liquids, bird-beak sign       |

4. ABCv1.1 Flashcards

  • Definition & Pathophys: GERD = chronic acid exposure due to LES dysfunction
  • Symptoms: Heartburn, regurgitation, worse lying down
  • Alarm Symptoms: Dysphagia, bleeding, weight loss → EGD
  • Diagnosis: Clinical ± PPI trial, EGD if alarm features or >60 y/o
  • Treatment: Lifestyle + PPI daily before breakfast
  • Complications: Barrett’s, strictures, aspiration

5. Clinical Triggers

| Clinical Clue                           | Think GERD?   |
|----------------------------------------|---------------|
| Burning chest pain after meals         | ✅             |
| Acid taste in mouth                    | ✅             |
| Worse when lying down                  | ✅             |
| Chronic cough without lung findings    | ✅             |
| No improvement with PPIs               | ❌ → check for EOE |

6. Extra Section (Clinical Pearls)

  • PPI trial is diagnostic in typical, low-risk patients
  • Always biopsy Barrett’s for dysplasia surveillance
  • Refractory symptoms → consider EOE or noncompliance
